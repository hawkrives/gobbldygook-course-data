PSCI 247B: Political Ethics and Public Policy (2005-2006, Semester 2)

Students survey the major traditions of ethical theory and their implications for public policy. Ethical theories are interspersed with case material throughout the course. The major focus is on the ethics of policy and the development of tools for the ethical assessment of policy alternatives. Applications range from health care, the environment, economic policy, welfare policy, education, affirmative action and crime.
