HIST 121: The Making of Modern Russia (2006-2007, Interim)

This course explores the origins of the modern Russian empire. Using primary sources such as folktales, legal codes, letters, and religious icons, students consider Russia's development from a loose collection of princedoms into a powerful, multi-ethnic empire spanning 11 time zones. Topics include the impact of geography and climate, the Orthodox religion, Mongol rule, gender roles, the rise of autocracy, and social rebellion.
