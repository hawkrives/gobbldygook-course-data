GCON 218B: The Tradition in Crisis: Dissenters and Defenders (2005-2006, Semester 2)

Revolutionary changes occurred in economics, politics, philosophy, aesthetics and women's roles at the beginning of the 19th century. Students consider the development of modern social and natural sciences and examine various attempts to restate the Western tradition in the face of continuing intellectual and social transformations. Students discuss Darwin, Marx, Freud, Nietzsche, Dostoyevsky, Woolf and King and the artistry of Beethoven, Ibsen, David and Picasso.
