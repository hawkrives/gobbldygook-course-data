ART 126: Thinking Visually (2005-2006, Summer Session 1)

This course explores what it means to be visually literate. Students work at what Edward Tufte describes as the "intersection of image, word, number, art." Assignments include observational drawing and the study of principles and strategies of design that are applicable to any field of interest. The course covers both theoretical and practical aspects of visual thinking.
