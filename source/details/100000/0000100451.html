ESTH 290: Sport Ethics in Society (1.0) (2015-2016, Semester 1)

Students examine the conceptual framework for ethical decision-making using sports as a prism to examine normative perspectives. Varied ethical perspectives are analyzed through the conflicts presented in sports-related questions. Course readings and lectures examine the theoretical constructs of ethical decision-making from Christian and non-Christian positions. Offered every year. Counts toward biomedical studies concentration (available to students through class of 2016). Prerequisite: completion of BTS-T or permission of instructor.
